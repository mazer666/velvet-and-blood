# Game Rules

This document defines the first draft of the character and rules direction. It is not a final system.

## Core Rule Direction

The game should be driven by choices, checks, consequences, inner voices, and social perception. The player should not choose a final supernatural path during character creation. Instead, the path develops through play.

## Character Categories

The character sheet should eventually contain three main categories.

### Geistig

Mental, investigative, interpretive, and memory-related voices.

Initial voices:

- **Die Ermittlerin**: Notices clues, contradictions, public records, and procedural details.
- **Die Tote**: Speaks from absence, dread, memory gaps, death certificates, and death-adjacent intuition.
- **Die Pedantin**: Cares about exactness, rules, documents, grammar, stamps, and improper forms.
- **Der Zweifel**: Questions motives, evidence, self-image, and convenient answers.

### Sozial

Social, performative, moral, vulgar, and conversational voices.

Initial voices:

- **Die Dame**: Reads manners, status, clothes, rooms, and polite violence.
- **Die Gosse**: Understands street logic, insults, hunger, and survival below respectability.
- **Die Anständige**: Worries about propriety, guilt, duty, and what decent people pretend not to do.
- **Die Freche**: Pushes boundaries, mocks authority, flirts with danger, and says the wrong thing usefully.

### Körperlich

Bodily, sensory, painful, hungry, and instinctive voices.

Initial voices:

- **Die Hungrige**: Tracks appetite, craving, blood, food, and the body as an argument.
- **Die Hand**: Handles touch, tools, gestures, theft, locks, and practical action.
- **Der Schmerz**: Registers injury, endurance, punishment, and useful suffering.
- **Das Tierchen**: Notices small movements, fear, warmth, fur, corners, and animal logic.

## Path Development

The three major paths are:

1. **Jägerin**: Occult investigator or huntress.
2. **Samtläuferin**: Feline shapeshifter.
3. **Vampirin**: Vampire involved in family intrigue.

The player must not choose Jägerin, Samtläuferin, or Vampirin during character creation. These paths should emerge from repeated choices, failed checks, relationships, physical changes, consequences, scenes, and occasional contradictory records. A player should discover what the character is becoming rather than select it from a menu at the start.

## Checks and Failure

Checks should produce story. Some checks should be final. Others may become repeatable if something meaningful changes, such as character attributes, story progress, new evidence, a different document, or a changed social situation. Every meaningful failure should change the world, the character, a clue, a rumor, or the way others see the protagonist.

A failed check may create:

- A clue discovered in an embarrassing way.
- A social enemy or debt.
- A physical scar or condition.
- A new rumor.
- A comedic disaster.
- An alternate entrance to a scene.
- A stronger pull toward one supernatural path.
- A police note, unpaid fine, witness statement, hospital observation, scandal sheet, or family dossier.

## First Frau Zwirndl Dialogue Direction

The first conversation with Frau Zwirndl must not be written as a simple confession scene. Frau Zwirndl presses toward truth about Lisel's disappearance, but the protagonist genuinely does not remember what happened. The scene should preserve uncertainty: Frau Zwirndl does not fully believe the protagonist, yet she does not dismiss the possibility of real memory loss.

Do not push the scene too early or too explicitly toward the vampire path. Avoid early certainty such as wrong pulse, unnatural coldness, corpse-like body, or clear undeath. Her observations should remain socially sharp and physically ambiguous, for example:

- “Ist Ihnen nicht gut? Sie sehen blass aus.”
- “Sie sehen aus, als hätte der Morgen Sie zurückgegeben und sofort bereut.”
- “Sie haben Erde am Saum, Blut am Mundwinkel und den Blick einer Frau, die gerade versucht, sich selbst höflich nicht zu kennen.”

The player should be able to answer Frau Zwirndl through six valid styles: honest confusion, inventing something out of embarrassment, deliberate lying, evasive self-protection, returning the question, or silence. None is the single correct answer. Each should alter tone, trust, inner voice reactions, available hints, and possible consequences rather than merely passing or failing the exchange.

Core line for Frau Zwirndl:

> “Ich will kein Geständnis. Geständnisse sind oft nur Lügen mit besserer Haltung. Ich will wissen, was wahr ist.”

Possible closing direction:

> “Fangen Sie bei dem an, was nicht weglaufen kann: Fleck, Knopf, Lieferzettel, Tür.”


## First Playable Investigation Mini-Loop

After Frau Zwirndl's first conversation, the player should be able to investigate the gutter and courtyard behind Café Morgenstern. This is the first playable mini-loop after dialogue and should combine crime-scene investigation with self-examination. Do not implement gameplay code yet; keep this as narrative/data direction.

Design goals:

- The player should not immediately solve the mystery.
- Clues should be small, contradictory, embarrassing, humorous, and unsettling.
- Failure should still create clues, complications, rumors, or consequences.
- The loop should ideally require or strongly encourage at least three gathered clues before returning to Frau Zwirndl or advancing the scene.
- All early evidence must remain ambiguous enough to support future **Jägerin**, **Samtläuferin**, or **Vampirin** development.

Primary interactables:

1. **Bloodstain**
   - Does not dry.
   - Too round and too orderly.
   - Must not immediately imply only the vampire path.
   - Possible voices: **Die Ermittlerin**, **Die Hungrige**, **Die Dame**.
   - Failure can reveal a clue through embarrassment, such as slipping or kneeling in something unpleasant.
2. **Brass button**
   - Does not belong to the protagonist's dress.
   - May show a small **FMoGH** emblem.
   - **FMoGH** means **“Fürsorgestelle für Minderjährige ohne gesicherten Hausstand”** and is usually called **“die Fürsorgestelle”**.
   - May have fallen from a gray coat or official-looking uniform.
3. **Delivery note**
   - Looks banal at first: coffee, milk, coal, bread.
   - Contains a partial official mark or note connected to FMoGH.
   - Possible phrases: “FMoGH — Sichtung erfolgt”, partial signature “… Windschütz”, “Minderjährige Hinterhofnutzung: vorläufig ungeklärt”, or “Bestätigung rückwärtiger Zugangsverhältnisse”.
   - Should feel bureaucratic and absurd, not like a magical scroll.
4. **Cellar door**
   - Looks ordinary but feels wrong.
   - May be missing from a nearby plan or delivery sketch.
   - Connects subtly to **Schwellenkunst**, the general threshold-ability concept.
   - In this first loop, Lisel's blocked ability should appear tied to the bureaucratic **“Bestätigung rückwärtiger Zugangsverhältnisse”**, so Mizzi and others can plausibly feel responsible for a mundane access failure.
   - Failure to open or inspect it should still reveal something, such as a child's ribbon, cloakroom token, or hidden scratch mark.
5. **Protagonist's pockets**
   - Contain no clear ID and no reliable name.
   - Possible contents: bent hairpin, unnamed receipt, piece of sugar, torn paper scrap with “freiwillig”, or a small object connected to Lisel such as a marble, ribbon, or cloakroom token.
   - Do not reveal the lost-name mechanism yet, but allow an unsettling echo of Lisel's later line: “Das ist nicht dein Name.”
   - The result should be funny, sad, and unsettling.

Optional supporting elements: torn dress, a face reflected in a dark window or puddle, and the cat on the rain barrel, which may nudge the brass button or simply judge the protagonist.

Possible Frau Zwirndl line after enough clues:

> “Drei Hinweise. Das ist in Aurelstein fast schon eine Persönlichkeit.”


## Schwellenkunst and Path-Specific Threshold Forms

Use **Schwellenkunst** as the general concept for threshold-related abilities: doors, hidden accesses, social borders, claims, names, debts, inheritances, bloodlines, and transitions. Do not present it as clean mechanics yet. It should feel like a half-understood occult/social practice. The three path-specific forms are:

- **Jägerin — Schwellenkunde:** reading doors, borders, access contradictions, legal gaps, and the traces left by impossible crossings.
- **Samtläuferin — Samtgang:** moving softly through physical, social, and territorial edges without reducing the path to generic shapeshifting.
- **Vampirin — Eintrittsrecht:** crossing through debt, inheritance, bloodline, obligation, possession, old promises, names, and family claims.

**Eintrittsrecht** must not use the standard invitation trope as its core. Vampiric access should not be based on being invited into a home. If a threshold opens to the vampire path, the pressure should come from debt, inheritance, bloodline, obligation, possession, old promises, family claims, names, and legal or social entitlement. A vampire does not merely ask to enter; she finds the claim that says she was already owed entry.

For Lisel, the surface explanation should initially be bureaucratic: **“Bestätigung rückwärtiger Zugangsverhältnisse”**. This lets Mizzi, staff, and bystanders feel plausibly responsible for a rear-access mistake. The deeper truth is that the protagonist's lost name was used as a false key or block against Lisel. That makes the guilt shared: Mizzi, the protagonist, the Fürsorgestelle, and possibly others each carry a partial responsibility. Lisel's line **“Das ist nicht dein Name”** points toward this deeper truth and should not be explained too early.

Frau Zwirndl's teaching should be unreliable by design. She knows her own threshold practice, but should not name it early. She initially deflects questions about Schwellenkunst with: **“Ich bin Garderobenfrau. Ich nehme Mäntel. Keine metaphysischen Beschwerden.”** Repeated pressure may make her explain her own cloakroom practice with partial accuracy while describing other threshold traditions through prejudice, gossip, and territorial suspicion. Do not have her casually say **Samtläufer**, **Jägerin**, or **Vampirin** too early. The game should let inner voices flag the bias without immediately correcting it. Reward recognizing the distortion with one of: an archive entry, a later dialogue option, or inner-voice resonance.

## Act 1 Opening Follow-Up Scenes

After the first courtyard investigation, expand the opening through three narrative beats: Frau Zwirndl's follow-up, Mizzi's confession, and the cloakroom sketch. Keep this as narrative/data guidance only. Do not implement gameplay code and do not choose an engine.

### Frau Zwirndl Follow-Up

Frau Zwirndl should gather the clues without solving them. She only appears old and harmless. She is not a neutral source and not a lore lecturer. Under pressure she may hint at old families, door debts, bloodlines, inheritances, superstitions, and people who read doors like crime scenes, but she remains guarded and biased.

### Mizzi Witness/Confession Scene

Mizzi is a waitress, secret witness, and partially responsible, not evil. She signed **“Bestätigung rückwärtiger Zugangsverhältnisse”**. The document did not name Lisel; it described a rear access, door, or place and made a protected place visible to the city. Mizzi thinks this allowed the Fürsorgestelle to act.

Mizzi gives two concrete hints:

1. Frau Chorrätin Windschütz came from **FMoGH**, wore grey or dove-grey official clothing with clean gloves and a small emblem, carried a dark leather folder, and used bureaucratic words like **“Minderjährige”**, **“Obhut”**, **“Überstellung”**, and **“Zugangsverhältnisse”**. Possible line: **“Niemand nimmt dem Kind etwas weg. Wir geben ihm eine Zuständigkeit.”**
2. Lisel could not use the cloakroom escape as expected. Hint, do not state, that she reached toward the impossible space between coats.

When Mizzi starts to cry, interrupt the emotional sentence with the steam boiler shrieking, then a guest complaining that the Sachertorte is dry. Mizzi may snap back: **“Dann begießen S’ sie mit Ihrer Enttäuschung!”**

### Zwirndl Demonstration and Seven-Door Sketch

Under pressure, Frau Zwirndl may reluctantly demonstrate that the cloakroom is not ordinary by pulling a full folder from a drawer far too small to contain it. This should look awkward and practical, not elegant. She should deny it: **“Alte Möbel. Die verziehen sich.”** or **“Das ist keine Schwellenkunst. Das ist schlechte Tischlerei mit Benehmen.”**

The folder contains a cloakroom sketch that first looks normal — entrance, counter, coat hooks, mirror, umbrella stand, staff passage, drawer wall — but shows seven marked doors. Possible labels: **Gästetür**, **Personaltür**, **Kellertür**, **Manteltür**, **Dienstbotentür**, **Katzentür**, **Siebte Tür**. Do not explain all seven doors. Let the sketch imply that Salon Siebte Tür is bound to places, routes, habits, and thresholds, not just people.

Lisel's mark on the sketch: **“Lisel war schneller”** beside a small drawing of a grinning mouth with a tooth gap. Frau Zwirndl should become briefly quiet and hide the emotion. Possible lines: **“Das hat sie draufgeschmiert.”** and **“Sie war nirgends oft. Sie war überall zu oft.”**

### Shared Guilt and Rewards

The scene should create shared guilt: Mizzi signed the access document, the protagonist lost or gave away her name, the Fürsorgestelle acted on the opening, Windschütz may know more than she admits, and the Salon failed to protect Lisel. Each character can believe they are guilty while hoping someone else is more guilty.

Reward detecting Zwirndl's biased knowledge with insight rather than blocking progress. Possible rewards: Akte entry **“Zwirndls Wissen hat Grenzen.”**, insight **“Die Garderobe hat sieben Wege.”**, or later dialogue option **“Sie wussten viel über Ihre Türen. Nicht über alle.”**

## Records and Dossiers

Aurelstein uses records as setting texture and story pressure. Documents can be clues, weapons, traps, lies, protections, or forms of imprisonment, but they should be used deliberately and sparingly. The game may later include dossier-like journal entries, but the core loop should remain choices, voices, social pressure, investigation, and consequence.

Possible future examples:

- The player finds three different names for herself in three different records.
- A failed social check causes a police clerk to open a file on her.
- A hospital record claims she died years ago.
- A church book lists her as never born.
- A debt ledger gives her access to someone else's secrets.
- A vampire family uses inheritance law as a supernatural weapon.
- A Samtläufer Salon hides its territory behind harmless-looking municipal property records.
- A hunter faction tracks monsters through inconsistencies in public files.

## Tracking, Inventory, and Resources

Future prototypes need documentation-friendly tracking for choices, checks, evidence, path pressure, open leads, closed leads, and dead-end prevention. Player decisions and checks should be documented so scenes can be tested for unresolved branches. This tracking can stay behind the scenes and does not need to become an in-world file mechanic.

The game will also need an inventory or resource-management concept for evidence, documents, money, favors, hunger, wounds, social debts, and other narrative resources.

## Protagonist Name

The player should not type a custom name. The protagonist's names should emerge through story, records, rumors, failures, false identities, and player choices. Different people and documents may call her by different names, which keeps later localization and possible voice-over feasible.

## Player Trust and Saves

A future robust save system is a core trust requirement. It should eventually support manual saves, autosaves, multiple save slots, visible save confirmation, savegame versioning, and protection against silent save failure.

## Future Audio Readiness

Dialogue and text should be data-driven and ID-based so future localization and possible voice-over can be added without rewriting scenes. Avoid arbitrary player-entered names because they make voiced dialogue and translated references harder to maintain.

## Combat

Combat is rare and not the core loop. Violence can exist, but the game should focus first on investigation, dialogue, pressure, shame, hunger, fear, witnesses, and consequence.
