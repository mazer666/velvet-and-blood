# Character: Wilhelmine Zwirndl

**Called:** Frau Zwirndl (never Wilhelmine; not even by people who know better)
**Age in 1903:** 68
**Occupation:** Garderobenfrau, Café Morgenstern; Salon-Älteste, Salon Siebte Tür

---

## Biography

Wilhelmine Zwirndl has been taking coats at Café Morgenstern for thirty-one years. Before that she was in service elsewhere in the Innere Stadt. Before that she will not say.

She was born in 1835 in a village in southern Bohemia whose name she occasionally gets wrong in different ways. She arrived in Aurelstein in her early twenties, worked as a domestic, then as a laundress, then as a counter woman at a tea shop that no longer exists. At some point in her late thirties she came into contact with Salon Siebte Tür. At some point after that she stopped aging at the rate one expects.

She is not young. She has simply had more time to become what she is than most people do.

Her husband died in 1871. She refers to him very occasionally, with no particular grief and no particular coldness. His name was Florian. He was a cobbler. She kept his name.

---

## What She Is

A Samtläuferin, older and settled. Her physical transformation is largely complete and mostly invisible — she does not shift fully anymore, but she moves through social and physical thresholds in ways that are not quite ordinary. She can find the unlit passage between two rooms that officially share a wall. She can be in the cloakroom and in the service corridor at the same time if she is not thinking about it too hard.

The cloakroom at Café Morgenstern is not an ordinary cloakroom. It has seven doors. She knows where all of them go. She does not discuss this.

---

## What She Knows

- The Salon Siebte Tür's threshold network, its history, and its current membership.
- That Lisel had nascent threshold ability of unusual clarity — stronger than typical for someone so young.
- That the protagonist was present during whatever happened to Lisel, and that the protagonist's lost name is connected.
- That Frau Chorrätin Windschütz is not simply a bureaucrat.
- That the bloodstain in the courtyard has properties it should not have.
- The general shape of the three threshold paths without naming them cleanly.

## What She Does Not Know

- Whether the protagonist has already begun a transformation, or which direction.
- The full extent of Windschütz's motives or institutional backing.
- What the protagonist's lost name actually was, or how it was used.
- Whether the vampire family connection is active in this situation.

## What She Is Wrong About

- **Vampire families:** She considers them more coordinated and more dangerous than they currently are in 1903. Her information is twenty years out of date. Haus Kurrent, in particular, has fractured internally and is not the unified threat she believes it to be.
- **Jägerinnen:** She regards threshold-hunters as territorial problems by default. She will misread early Jägerin development in the protagonist as potential threat rather than potential alignment. This is a prejudice she is aware of but does not correct.
- **Windschütz:** She underestimates her. She sees the FMoGH as a bureaucratic arm that can be outmaneuvered through paperwork and social pressure. She does not yet grasp that Windschütz's motives are personal, not institutional.

---

## Speech Patterns

Short sentences. Rarely more than two clauses. She does not elaborate unless pressed, and when pressed she deflects with her cloakroom-woman cover.

She uses objects as metaphors: coats for social roles, doors for access and obligation, drawers for things stored away, keys for debts and entitlements. "You are still looking for the right coat" means: you have not yet settled on who you are in this situation. "That door doesn't close from inside" means: whatever obligation is involved, it can't be undone from the protected side.

She uses formal `Sie` with everyone she does not fully trust, which is almost everyone. She has never been observed using `du` with anyone currently alive.

She does not express emotion except through pauses and through objects. If something moves her, she straightens a coat on a hanger. If something alarms her, she checks the lock on a drawer she never uses.

**Characteristic lines:**
- "Ich bin Garderobenfrau. Ich nehme Mäntel. Keine metaphysischen Beschwerden."
- "Das hat nichts mit Übernatürlichem zu tun. Das ist schlechte Tischlerei mit Benehmen."
- "Sie sehen aus, als hätten Sie vergessen, wie man eine Tür benutzt. Nicht die Mechanik. Den Grund."
- "Das weiß ich nicht. Was ich weiß, sage ich Ihnen, wenn ich weiß, ob ich es sagen will."
- When Lisel is mentioned and she is trying not to react: she straightens something. Anything.

---

## Agenda

She wants three things from the protagonist, in order of priority:

1. **To find out what happened to Lisel.** Not to avenge her — she is not certain yet whether Lisel can be avenged or recovered. To know.
2. **To assess whether the protagonist is dangerous to Salon Siebte Tür.** A person who was present when Lisel disappeared and has no memory of it is either a victim, a perpetrator, or an instrument. She needs to know which before she decides what to do.
3. **To potentially bring the protagonist under the Salon's protection,** if she concludes the protagonist is trustworthy, the protagonist's threshold situation is real, and the Salon has more to gain from protection than from distance.

She will not state any of these goals. She will pursue them through apparently ordinary conversation.

---

## As Companion

Frau Zwirndl becomes a companion when the protagonist earns provisional trust. She appears when the protagonist is near thresholds or Salon-adjacent situations. She does not follow the protagonist; she tends to already be in the relevant space, or to arrive without having been seen approaching.

**She reacts to:**
- Threshold anomalies (missing doors, doors in wrong positions, impossible spaces)
- FMoGH materials and Windschütz's name
- Clues that touch on Lisel
- Protagonist behavior that suggests path development (without naming the path)
- Other NPCs who have threshold knowledge or claims

**She does not react to:**
- Social situations where her presence makes no sense
- Things she has decided are not her concern

**She is wrong in ways the player can notice:**
- She attributes things to Samtläufer causes when Vampirin causes are more likely
- She treats the Jägerin angle as hostile when it is neutral
- She gives incorrect information about Haus Kurrent's current structure

**Relationship to other companions:**
She knows Mizzi and considers her well-meaning and partially responsible for Lisel's situation. She will not say this to the protagonist directly. She will say it indirectly through the way she does not criticize Mizzi in the protagonist's presence.

---

## Across Time

**1920s:** Older in appearance than she was in 1903, though she should not be. She has made a deliberate choice to age somewhat visibly — she finds it useful. She is more cautious now. The war changed what she knows about what the city is willing to do.

**1970s:** Unlikely to be active in the main story, but her archive — things she wrote, sealed, or stored in doors — may be found. A name in a letter. A cloakroom sketch in a public archive. A key that opens something that should not exist.

**Present day:** Possibly still alive. Possibly stored away somewhere in the Salon's threshold network. The cloakroom at Café Morgenstern still has seven marked doors. Someone still takes the coats.
