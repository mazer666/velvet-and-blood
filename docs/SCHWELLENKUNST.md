# Schwellenkunst

Schwellenkunst is the general concept for threshold-related abilities: doors, hidden accesses, social borders, claims, names, debts, inheritances, bloodlines, and transitions. Do not present it as clean public mechanics. It should feel like a half-understood occult and social practice. The three path-specific forms are:

- **Jägerin — Schwellenkunde:** reading doors, borders, access contradictions, legal gaps, and the traces left by impossible crossings.
- **Samtläuferin — Samtgang:** moving softly through physical, social, and territorial edges without reducing the path to generic shapeshifting.
- **Vampirin — Eintrittsrecht:** crossing through debt, inheritance, bloodline, obligation, possession, old promises, names, and family claims.

## Eintrittsrecht

Eintrittsrecht must not use the standard invitation trope as its core. Vampiric access should not be based on being invited into a home. If a threshold opens to the vampire path, the pressure should come from debt, inheritance, bloodline, obligation, possession, old promises, family claims, names, and legal or social entitlement. A vampire does not merely ask to enter; she finds the claim that says she was already owed entry.

## Lisel and the Threshold Block

For Lisel, the surface explanation should initially be bureaucratic: **"Bestätigung rückwärtiger Zugangsverhältnisse"**. This lets Mizzi, staff, and bystanders feel plausibly responsible for a mundane access failure. The deeper truth is that the protagonist's lost name was used as a false key or block against Lisel. That makes the guilt shared: Mizzi, the protagonist, the Fürsorgestelle, and possibly others each carry a partial responsibility. Lisel's line **"Das ist nicht dein Name"** points toward this deeper truth and should not be explained too early.

## Frau Zwirndl's Unreliable Teaching

Frau Zwirndl knows her own threshold practice but should not name it early. She initially deflects questions with: **"Ich bin Garderobenfrau. Ich nehme Mäntel. Keine metaphysischen Beschwerden."**

Repeated pressure may make her explain her own cloakroom practice with partial accuracy while describing other threshold traditions through prejudice, gossip, and territorial suspicion. Do not have her casually name **Samtläufer**, **Jägerin**, or **Vampirin** as clean taxonomy. The game should let inner voices flag the bias without immediately correcting it.

Detecting this distortion should be rewarded with one of: an archive entry, a later dialogue option, or inner-voice resonance.

Inner voices that may notice the bias:

- **Die Ermittlerin**: hears missing evidence in her claims.
- **Die Pedantin**: objects to incorrect categories.
- **Der Zweifel**: recognizes that Zwirndl is protecting a border of her own.
