# Lore Bible

This is a first structured lore bible. It should stay short until the project has a playable prototype.

## Aurelstein

Aurelstein is a fictional imperial city in 1903. It has coffeehouses, ministries, salons, churches, canals, slaughterhouses, archives, rented rooms, and courtyards that remember too much.

The city is inspired by the social atmosphere of fin-de-siècle Central Europe, but it is not real Vienna and should not copy any existing fictional setting.

## Aurelstein Remembers in Paper

The city remembers through paper, seals, ledgers, police reports, birth records, debt registers, hospital files, death certificates, church books, inheritance papers, house records, and private dossiers.

The archives are not neutral. They can protect, erase, accuse, invent, misclassify, or resurrect a person on paper. The player character does not only lack memory; the city may have records about her, and those records may contradict each other. This motif should stay sharp and meaningful rather than appearing in every scene.

## The Year 1903

The year 1903 gives the setting a pressure point between old aristocratic structures, modern bureaucracy, labor politics, new sciences, occult fashion, religious guilt, and urban poverty.

Historical texture is welcome, but Aurelstein remains a fictional city with its own institutions and supernatural rules.

## The Nameless Woman

The player character begins without a stable public identity. She wakes behind a coffeehouse near a bloodstain that does not dry. Her future path is shaped by choices and failures during play, not by a fixed class selection at character creation.

Her identity may emerge through conflicting documents, rumors, failures, false identities, and choices. Different people and documents may call her by different names. The player should not type a custom name.

## Long-Term Act Structure

- **Act 1: 1903 — Awakening, City, First Truth** is the first playable MVP and the only act developed at the beginning.
- **Act 2: 1920s — Interwar Years** is a future expansion candidate. Aurelstein has changed politically, socially, and culturally. Do not detail it yet.
- **Act 3: 1970s — Postwar Ghosts** is a future expansion candidate. The city carries buried guilt, reconstruction, silence, inherited crimes, and institutional memory. Do not detail it yet.
- **Act 4: Present Day — The City Remembers** is a future expansion candidate. Long-term consequences of earlier choices, identities, documents, betrayals, and alliances may become visible. Do not detail it yet.

## Supernatural Paths

### Jägerin

An occult investigator or huntress who learns to read traces, lies, ritual leftovers, social contradictions, and inconsistencies in public files. This path should emerge through play, not character creation.

### Samtläuferin

A feline shapeshifter whose nature is tied to stealth, instinct, velvet-footed movement, appetite, territory, and social performance. A Samtläuferin is not a werewolf and does not follow borrowed shapeshifter rules. This path should emerge through choices, failures, relationships, physical changes, and records.

### Vampirin

A vampire bound to old family politics, inheritance, hunger, documents, favors, debts, and the question of who is allowed to be remembered. This path should emerge gradually through consequences, relationships, bodily signs, and family records.

## Frau Zwirndl and Salon Siebte Tür

**Frau Zwirndl** appears to be the old cloakroom woman of **Café Morgenstern**. This should remain a useful social disguise rather than a simple explanation of her authority. She belongs to **Salon Siebte Tür**, a Samtläufer circle with access to gossip, thresholds, service corridors, coats, keys, favors, and things overheard by people the respectable world forgets to notice.

In the first conversation, Frau Zwirndl knows that **Lisel** is missing, that **FMoGH** and **Frau Chorrätin Windschütz** were involved in the night's events, and that the protagonist was somehow connected. She does not yet know whether the protagonist was culprit, victim, witness, or tool. She wants truth, not a neat confession, and she can believe two things at once: that the protagonist is hiding something, and that the protagonist may genuinely remember nothing.

## Samtläufer Salons

A **Salon** is the basic social unit for Samtläufer. A Salon may act like a refuge, gossip network, finishing school, den, conspiracy, or theatrical disaster depending on its members.

Salons should feel intimate and political rather than militarized. A Salon may hide its territory behind harmless-looking municipal property records.

## Vampire Families

### Haus Kurrent

Associated with archives, registers, municipal offices, old scripts, stamped permissions, missing files, forged identities, and bureaucratic immortality. Haus Kurrent understands that a person can be killed, preserved, or replaced by paperwork.

### Haus Rabenau

Associated with aristocracy, aesthetics, wealth, inheritance, social ownership, and beautiful forms of possession.

### Haus Heiligenlicht

Associated with religion, guilt, sanctity, holy hypocrisy, confession, church books, and public virtue.

### Haus Kaltwasser

Associated with workers, canals, slaughterhouses, practical violence, cold logistics, and survival without ornament.

### Haus Krummherz

Associated with monstrous appearances, public fear, misunderstanding, and unexpected compassion toward the lost and transformed.
