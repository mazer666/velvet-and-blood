# Act 1 Opening Sequence

Concise outline for the opening after the first courtyard investigation. This is narrative/design documentation only: no gameplay code, no engine choice, and no final script.

## 1. Courtyard Investigation Recap

After Frau Zwirndl's first conversation, the player investigates the gutter and courtyard behind **Café Morgenstern**. The scene should ideally return with at least three clues: the too-round bloodstain, the brass FMoGH button, the delivery note, the wrong-feeling cellar door, or the protagonist's pockets.

The clues must remain contradictory and incomplete. They should suggest crime, bureaucracy, shame, hidden access, lost identity, and possible occult pressure without proving a path.

## 2. Return to Frau Zwirndl

Frau Zwirndl gathers the clues without solving them:

> “Drei Hinweise. Das ist in Aurelstein fast schon eine Persönlichkeit.”

She is not a lore lecturer. If pressed about the cloakroom, doors, or Schwellenkunst, she first hides behind her role:

> “Ich bin Garderobenfrau. Ich nehme Mäntel. Keine metaphysischen Beschwerden.”

She knows her own threshold practice, but she should not name **Samtläufer**, **Jägerin**, or **Vampirin** this early. She may hint at old families, door debts, bloodlines, inheritances, superstitions, and people who read doors like crime scenes, but her language remains guarded, biased, and incomplete.

## 3. Mizzi Confession Scene

**Mizzi** is a waitress, secret witness, and partial participant rather than a villain. She signed a document called **“Bestätigung rückwärtiger Zugangsverhältnisse”**. It did not name Lisel; it described a rear access, door, or place. Mizzi believes this made a protected place visible to the city and allowed the Fürsorgestelle to act.

Mizzi gives two concrete hints:

1. **Frau Chorrätin Windschütz** came from the **Fürsorgestelle für Minderjährige ohne gesicherten Hausstand** (**FMoGH**), usually just **die Fürsorgestelle**. She was calm, polite, official, frighteningly certain, in a grey or dove-grey suit with clean gloves, a small FMoGH emblem, and a dark leather folder. She preferred words like “Minderjährige”, “Obhut”, “Überstellung”, and “Zugangsverhältnisse”. Possible line: “Niemand nimmt dem Kind etwas weg. Wir geben ihm eine Zuständigkeit.”
2. Lisel could not use the cloakroom escape as expected. Mizzi only saw that Lisel wanted between the coats; Frau Zwirndl understood more than she said.

Mizzi's breakdown should be guilty and absurd, not melodramatic:

> “Ich wollte doch nur—”

The steam boiler shrieks and ruins the sentence.

Inner voice option:

> “Endlich sagt hier jemand ehrlich, wie diese Stadt klingt.”

Then a guest calls:

> “Fräulein! Die Sachertorte ist trocken!”

Mizzi, still crying, snaps back:

> “Dann begießen S’ sie mit Ihrer Enttäuschung!”

## 4. Zwirndl Threshold Demonstration

Under pressure, Frau Zwirndl accidentally or reluctantly proves the cloakroom is not ordinary. She opens a drawer barely larger than a shoebox, reaches in far too deeply, loses her arm up to the shoulder, briefly gets part of her head stuck, mutters at the contents, and pulls out a full folder that could not possibly fit.

She immediately denies anything unusual:

- “Alte Möbel. Die verziehen sich.”
- “Das ist keine Schwellenkunst. Das ist schlechte Tischlerei mit Benehmen.”

This should feel practical, awkward, and absurd rather than elegant magic.

## 5. Seven-Door Cloakroom Sketch

The folder contains a sketch of the cloakroom of **Café Morgenstern**. At first it looks mundane: entrance, counter, coat hooks, mirror, umbrella stand, staff passage, and small drawer wall. Then the player notices seven marked doors, only one or two of which are normally visible.

Possible labels:

- Gästetür
- Personaltür
- Kellertür
- Manteltür
- Dienstbotentür
- Katzentür
- Siebte Tür

Do not explain all seven doors as a lecture. The sketch should imply that **Salon Siebte Tür** is bound to places, routes, habits, and thresholds, not just people.

Add Lisel's childish mark:

> “Lisel war schneller”

Next to it: a small drawing of a grinning mouth with a tooth gap. Frau Zwirndl goes briefly quiet and tries to hide it.

Possible lines:

- “Das hat sie draufgeschmiert.”
- “Sie war nirgends oft. Sie war überall zu oft.”

## 6. Hints Around Lisel's Blocked Escape

Lisel is a tooth-gapped street girl under Salon Siebte Tür's protection. She can find doors that officially do not exist and sometimes knows true names without understanding why that is dangerous. She was taken by the Fürsorgestelle.

Her early remembered line is:

> “Das ist nicht dein Name.”

Visible suspicion: Mizzi's signed access document exposed a protected rear access or place. Deeper truth, only hinted: the protagonist's lost or given-away name was used as a false key or block against Lisel.

Hint the likely cloakroom escape without naming it as certainty:

> “Sie griff nicht nach der sichtbaren Garderobentür. Sie griff dorthin, wo zwischen zwei Mänteln manchmal mehr Platz war, als der Raum erlaubte.”

Or:

> “Mizzi sah nur, dass Lisel zwischen die Mäntel wollte. Frau Zwirndl sah, welche Tür sie meinte.”

## 7. Open Mysteries and Player-Facing Clues

Keep the occult taxonomy hidden. Most people in Aurelstein know folk ideas — vampires, werewolves, ghosts, fairies, kobolds, witches, revenants, and local superstition — but precise terms like **Samtläufer**, **Samtgang**, **Schwellenkunde**, and **Eintrittsrecht** are not public knowledge.

Player-facing clues:

- Akte: “Zwirndls Wissen hat Grenzen.”
- Insight: “Die Garderobe hat sieben Wege.”
- Later dialogue option: “Sie wussten viel über Ihre Türen. Nicht über alle.”

Possible inner voice reactions:

- **Die Ermittlerin**: “Sie weiß, was sie tut. Sie weiß nicht, was die anderen tun. Ihre Spötteleien sind Landkarten mit fehlenden Straßen.”
- **Die Pedantin**: “Eine Garderobe mit sieben Türen ist nicht kompliziert. Sie ist baurechtlich beleidigend.”
- **Der Zweifel**: “Sie sagt ‘Garderobe’, meint aber Revier.”
- **Die Freche**: “Frag, ob die Mäntel auch Mitgliedsbeiträge zahlen.”
