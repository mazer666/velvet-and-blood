# Project Core

## Title

**Velvet and Blood – A City That Remembers**

## Genre

Occult narrative RPG with investigation, social pressure, grotesque comedy, supernatural transformation, and bureaucratic unease.

## Pitch

A nameless woman wakes in a gutter behind a coffeehouse in Aurelstein, 1903. She has no clear memory. Nearby, a bloodstain refuses to dry. The scene looks like a crime scene, but it first plays as a public humiliation full of social danger, bad assumptions, and witnesses. Her name is not typed by the player; it emerges through story, rumors, failures, false identities, choices, and occasional records. Through choices, failures, and consequences, she may become an occult investigator, a Samtläuferin, or a vampire caught in family intrigue.

## Setting

Aurelstein is a fictional imperial city inspired by the tensions, elegance, bureaucracy, poverty, and cultural contradictions of Vienna in 1903. It is not a historical simulation. It is an original city built for narrative play.

## Aurelstein Remembers in Paper

Memory is administrative, social, legal, religious, and financial as well as personal or supernatural. Police reports, hospital records, debt registers, church books, inheritance papers, house records, and private dossiers may protect the player, accuse her, erase her, or invent a version of her that never existed. This should remain a setting theme and story tool, not an overused mechanic.

## Tone

The humor may be absurd, argumentative, socially observant, grotesque, and politically sharp without copying specific characters, catchphrases, dialogue patterns, running jokes, scenes, factions, or mechanics from existing works.

The tone should prioritize:

1. Black humor.
2. Grotesque absurdity.
3. Political sharpness.
4. Horror underneath ordinary life.
5. Melancholy as a quieter fourth priority, never the only mood.

## Core Fantasy

The player is not a chosen hero. She is a suspicious survivor in a city of salons, offices, alleys, churches, gutters, and locked family archives. She survives by noticing contradictions, embarrassing the powerful, failing strangely, and becoming something the city did not plan for.

## Player Experience

Players should feel that every choice leaves a trace. Success reveals one kind of truth; failure reveals another. Every meaningful failure should change the world, the character, a clue, a rumor, or the way others see the protagonist. The game should reward curiosity, social risk, occult suspicion, and attention to language, gestures, witnesses, paperwork, and missing records.

## Long-Term Act Vision

The game may eventually span more than a century, but development must begin small.

- **Act 1: 1903 — Awakening, City, First Truth** is the MVP scope and the only act to develop at the beginning.
- **Act 2: 1920s — Interwar Years** is a future expansion candidate about a changed Aurelstein. Do not detail it yet.
- **Act 3: 1970s — Postwar Ghosts** is a future expansion candidate about buried guilt, silence, and institutional memory. Do not detail it yet.
- **Act 4: Present Day — The City Remembers** is a future expansion candidate where long-term consequences may become visible. Do not detail it yet.
